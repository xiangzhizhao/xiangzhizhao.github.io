Carbon Market Pilot Policies and Corporate Green Governance Performance: Evidence from China (Contemporary Economic Policy, Published)
Published Version | Working PDF
Abstract

This study employs a multi-period difference-in-differences model to analyze corporate data from 2009–2022 and assess the impact of China's carbon market pilot policy on corporate green governance performance, including environmental disclosure, green innovation, and stakeholder engagement. The policy improves green governance through two primary channels: alleviating corporate financing constraints and stimulating green technology innovation. The results remain robust under propensity-score-matching difference-in-differences estimation and spatial placebo tests. The effects are particularly strong among non-state-owned enterprises, firms located in China's central region, and firms led by environmentally proactive executives. The findings support the use of tiered carbon pricing and targeted green financing policies to address regional and ownership disparities and provide guidance for strengthening market-based green governance in emerging economies.
