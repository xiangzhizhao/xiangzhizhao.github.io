Hi, I’m Xiangzhi!
Pronunciation: Shee-ahng-jir

I am a PhD Student in Economics at Indiana University Bloomington.

Research Interests:
International Trade, Economic Geography, Labor Mobility, Regional Adjustment

My research focuses on international trade and economic geography. I study the sources of bilateral trade imbalances, the role of trade costs and policy distortions, and how regions adjust to economic shocks. My work combines structural trade models with empirical methods to better understand the mechanisms underlying global trade patterns.

Outside of research, I enjoy photography, pottery, and exploring new places through travel.
